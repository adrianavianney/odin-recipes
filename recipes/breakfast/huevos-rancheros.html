Huevos Rancheros

Huevos Ranchero served on top of a tortilla and a light spread of beans.

Rancher's Eggs

Huevos Rancheros is another simple and fast, yet packed full of flavor, classic served throughout Mexico. I find myself making this myself a lot. It's a recipe I picked up soon after moving out of my house, and have made it at least once a week since. You can say that this was the recipe that brought me into adulthood. I feel like we all have that one recipe we had over and over when we were first on our own and this was the one for me. It introduced me to the classic three ingredients that I have continously used throughout the years as I explored Mexican cuisin deeper.

I love the combination of flavors and aromas created by the ingredients in this recipe. It is truly amazing the variety of dishes that can be created with just a few ingredients. The simplicty of the dish allows it to be paired with other ingredients found throughout many Mexican dishes. Things like cilantro, avocado and sour cream have enhanced our food for centuries. This dish consists of a toasted tortilla, topped with a fried egg and accompanied with a side of beans. The finishing touches come with a cilantro garnish and a dollop of sour cream.

Ingredients

Sauce

  • 3 tomatoes
  • 3 chiles serranos(serrano peppers)
  • 1/4 white onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • salt to taste

Other Ingredients

  • 8 corn tortillas
  • 8 eggs
  • Salt to taste for eggs
  • Oil to fry eggs and tortillas
  • 2 cups of homemade fried beans

How To Make

Sauce

  1. In a pan over medium heat, toast the tomatoes, chiles, onion and garlic.
  2. Remove skin from tomatoes and chiles and place all ingredients in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium heat. Carefully pour sauce into pot to cook for about five minutes. Stirr as needed.

Assembling The Base

  1. While sauce is being cooked, in a separate pan place enough oil to fry the tortillas. Carefully place the tortillas in the pan and turn once each side is golden and crispy. Repeat to all 8 tortillas ensuring each side of the tortilla gets a light but even coat of oil.
  2. Now continue to fry your eggs.
  3. Place one of your fried tortillas on a place, cover it in a layer of beans, place one of your eggs on top and finish it up by covering it in the salsa.

Note: Top with sour cream, diced onion and cilantro for extra flavor.
